## 3. Sponsorship Tier Structure (What to Offer Sponsors)

Based on research across AAAI, MLH, Los Altos Hacks, HackUTA, and other events, here is a recommended tier structure adapted for your event:

### Recommended Tiers

| Tier | Price / In-Kind Value | Benefits |
|------|----------------------|----------|
| **Partner (Platinum)** | $5,000+ or equivalent in API credits | Everything below + keynote/workshop slot (30 min) + judge seat + branded challenge/prize track + VIP networking + logo on t-shirts + "Powered by [Sponsor]" branding + resume/GitHub access to participants + post-event demo access |
| **Gold** | $2,000-3,000 or equivalent | Everything below + workshop/tech talk slot + mentor presence at event + logo on website (prominent) + social media feature + swag distribution |
| **Silver** | $500-1,000 or equivalent | Logo on website + mention in opening/closing ceremonies + logo on event signage + social media mention |
| **API Credit Sponsor** | In-kind API credits ($1,250-2,500 value) | Custom package — logo placement, workshop slot, "Built with [Provider]" track, access to winning demos |

### Key Principle
For AI companies, you're not asking for cash. You're asking for API credits — which cost them significantly less than face value. A $2,500 credit grant from Anthropic or OpenAI costs them pennies on the dollar in compute. Frame it this way.

### What Sponsors Actually Want (by department)

| Who to Contact | What They Want | What to Offer |
|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| **Developer Relations** | Product adoption, developer feedback, community goodwill | Workshop slot, "Built with X" challenge track, usage data/feedback |
| **Recruiting/HR** | Talent pipeline, campus presence | Resume access, networking session, interview opportunities |
| **Marketing** | Brand awareness, content, social proof | Logo placement, social media, blog post about event |

## 5. Outreach Strategy — What Works

### The MLH-Recommended Process

1. **Build your prospect list** in a spreadsheet (not a CRM — overkill for hackathons)
2. **Find the right contact** — DevRel, Developer Advocacy, or Community leads (not generic info@ emails)
3. **Send a short, personal first email** — NO attachments, NO links, NO images (corporate spam filters block these)
4. **Follow up 1-2 weeks later** with your prospectus attached
5. **Goal of first email:** Get a phone/video call, not close the deal
6. **Conversion rate:** ~1 sponsor per 50 cold emails (but warm intros convert much higher)

### Email Tools for Finding Contacts
- **Hunter.io** — Find email addresses by domain
- **Lusha** — Chrome extension, high accuracy
- **Norbert** — 50 free searches
- **LinkedIn** — Search for "Developer Relations" or "Developer Advocacy" at target companies

### Email Timing
- Best days: Tuesday or Thursday mornings
- Best time: 8 AM in the recipient's timezone

### First Email Template (Adapted for Your Event)

```
Subject: 100 Romanian Students Building AI Agents — May 15-17

Hi [Name],

I'm [Your Name], organizing an agentic AI hackathon for ~100 high school
students in Timisoara, Romania. It's part of olimpiada-ai.ro, co-organized
with Politehnica University and West University of Timisoara.

Over 3 days (May 15-17), teams will build autonomous AI agents using LLM
APIs — not chatbots, but systems that reason, plan, use tools, and take
multi-step actions.

We're looking for API credit sponsors to power the event. For most of
these students, it'll be their first time working with [Claude/GPT/Gemini]
APIs. We'd love to discuss how [Company] could be involved.

Would you have 15 minutes for a call next week?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Follow-Up Email (1-2 Weeks Later)

```
Subject: Re: 100 Romanian Students Building AI Agents — May 15-17

Hi [Name],

Following up on my earlier note. I've attached our sponsorship prospectus
with details on the event and partnership tiers.

Quick summary of what we're looking for:
- API credits ($50-100 per team, ~25 teams)
- Optional: workshop slot, mentor presence, judge seat

Happy to hop on a call whenever works for you.

[Your Name]
```

## 7. Prospectus Best Practices

Based on MLH's organizer guide and successful hackathon examples:

1. **Keep it to 2-3 pages.** Sponsors don't read long decks.
2. **Include:** Event name, date, venue, expected attendance, website, contact email.
3. **Tell your story:** Who you are, why this hackathon matters, what makes it different.
4. **Three tiers** with cumulative benefits. Highest tier should not exceed 25% of total budget.
5. **Always offer custom packages** — include your email for inquiries.
6. **Under-promise, over-deliver** — cite conservative attendance figures.
7. **Review real examples:** Los Altos Hacks prospectus at https://www.losaltoshacks.com/2018/docs/sponsorship.pdf and AAAI 2025 at https://aaai.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/Hackathon-Sponsorship-2025.pdf
